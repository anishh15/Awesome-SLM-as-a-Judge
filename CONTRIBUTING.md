# Contributing to SLM-as-a-Judge Survey

Thank you for your interest in contributing to this repository! We aim to maintain a comprehensive and up-to-date collection of research on Small Language Models as Judges.

## How to Contribute

### Adding a Paper

1. **Fork** this repository.
2. Add the paper entry to the appropriate section in `README.md` using this format:

```markdown
| Paper Title | Venue | Year | [[pdf]](arxiv_link) [[code]](github_link) |
```

3. If the paper introduces a new benchmark or dataset, also add it to the **Benchmarks & Datasets** section.
4. Submit a **Pull Request** with a brief description of the paper and why it's relevant.

### Fixing Issues

- If you notice a broken link, incorrect venue/year, or misclassification, please open an issue or submit a PR directly.

### Suggesting New Categories

- If you believe a new category should be added to the taxonomy, please open an issue describing:
  - The proposed category name
  - Why it's distinct from existing categories
  - At least 3 papers that would fall under this category

## Guidelines

- **Relevance**: Papers should be directly related to using language models (especially small ones) as automated evaluators/judges.
- **Quality**: Prioritize peer-reviewed papers, but high-quality arXiv preprints are welcome.
- **Accuracy**: Double-check all links, venue names, and years before submitting.
- **Neutrality**: Follow the survey's principle of representing the state of the field, not promoting any single work.

## Code of Conduct

Please be respectful and constructive in all interactions. We are committed to maintaining a welcoming and inclusive community.

## Questions?

Feel free to reach out by opening an issue or contacting the maintainers.
